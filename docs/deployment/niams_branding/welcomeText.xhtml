The Osteoarthritis Initiative (OAI) is a multi-center, nine-year observational study of men and women, sponsored by the National Institutes of Health (part of the Department of Health & Human Services). The goals of the OAI are to help us better understand how to prevent and treat knee osteoarthritis, one of the most common causes of disability in adults.

This website contains the permanent image archive of the radiographs and magnetic resonance images acquired during this study. The website is made possible by the NBIA Image Archive system.

Available Quantitatively Analyzed Images:

  • Quantitative joint space width from longitudinal sets of knee X-rays (24-month, 12-month, baseline; various subsets of progression cohort and subset of non-exposed control cohort)
  • Quantitative cartilage and bone morphology from longitudinal sets of MRIs (48-month, 24-month, 12-month, baseline; various subsets of progression cohort)
  • Quantitative knee alignment and lower limb geometry from full-limb X-rays at a single time point (12-month; subset of progression cohort)

Available Semi-quantitatively Scored Images:

  • Semi-quantitative Kellgren-Lawrence grade and individual radiographic features of from longitudinal sets of fixed flexion knee X-rays (36-month, 24-month, 12-month, baseline; various subsets of progression cohort and subset of non-exposed control cohort)
